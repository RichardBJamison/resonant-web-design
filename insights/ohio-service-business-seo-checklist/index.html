SEO + Local Search · Ohio

SEO checklist for Ohio service businesses that need leads, not vanity traffic

Use this when you launch or rebuild a site for a landscaper, restaurant, trades operator, or service business in Cleveland, Northeast Ohio, or statewide. It is the same discipline we ship inside SEO Foundations—not a keyword dump.

Start a fit review SEO Foundations

1. Technical baseline (before content volume)

  • HTTPS, fast TTFB, mobile layout that does not hide the offer
  • One clear H1, unique title + meta per indexable URL
  • XML sitemap submitted; no soft-duplicate routes returning the homepage
  • robots.txt allows Googlebot; AI crawl policy intentional (do not leave Managed AI Disallow if you want AI citations)
  • Canonicals correct; no accidental noindex on money pages
  • Core pages linked from nav/footer—not orphaned

2. Local / Google Business Profile

  • Primary category matches the real business (Website designer / Landscaper / Restaurant—not vanity)
  • NAP consistent with the website footer
  • Service areas real (Cleveland, NE Ohio, Ohio)—no fake storefronts
  • Weekly GBP post with a real offer or proof, not stock spam
  • Review asks from real customers; never buy stars
  • Photos of real work, crew, or results—not only logo tiles

3. Page architecture that earns commercial intent

  • Home states who you help + where + what happens next
  • Service pages for money services (not one mega-page)
  • Location language that matches real service area without city doorway spam
  • Proof: projects, process, outcomes—screenshots when possible
  • Clear CTA to a conversion path (quote, book, fit review)—not “contact us someday”
  • Internal links: home → services/industries → start

4. Integrations bridge (when SEO alone is not enough)

Search that fills a voicemail box is wasted. For operators who miss calls, connect the site to intake:

  • System integrations — CRM, forms, analytics, automation
  • AI phone — answer, qualify, route, log notes
  • Measure: form submits, booked calls, /start/ completions—not just sessions

5. Hospitality & landscaping examples

Landscaping: service pages (mowing, hardscape, seasonal cleanup), portfolio proof, quote path, seasonal content. See landscaping website design in Ohio.

Restaurants / hospitality: menu or offer paths, booking/reservation links, local proof, ops-aware marketing—not brochure fluff. See restaurant & hospitality web design. Free ops tools we ship: Open Source Barware / IHS.

6. AI answer readiness (AEO basics)

  • Plain-text llms.txt entity file when you want AI systems to cite you correctly
  • FAQ schema only when FAQs are real on-page answers
  • Clear entity language: who you are, where you serve, what you sell
  • No thin AI filler pages built only to rank city names
