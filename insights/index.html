Editorial standard

Google does not need another pile of generic SEO articles.

Every article, checklist, category hub, and guide should be built around a specific decision a business owner or operator needs to make: what page to build, what proof to show, what search intent to serve, what system to connect, and how the work will be measured after launch.

The publishing system follows Google Search Central fundamentals: helpful people-first content, descriptive titles, crawlable internal links, structured data where it clarifies the page, clean canonicals, fast pages, and no doorway-page games. Local SEO pages need real service-area substance. AI and systems content needs screenshots, workflow logic, and operational proof when possible.

Backend SEO infrastructure

Each publishable page needs more than words.

The infrastructure behind Insights is the advantage: templates for titles and descriptions, category structure, article schema, BreadcrumbList schema, sitemap inclusion, canonical URLs, internal links, social images, author signals, and reusable calls to action.

  • Search intent: one clear query family and one clear reader problem.
  • Original proof: examples, screenshots, process notes, or client/operator context where available.
  • Clean crawl path: linked from a hub, included in sitemap, and connected to the right service page.
  • Structured data: Article, BreadcrumbList, FAQ, or Service schema only when it accurately describes the page.
  • Conversion path: every guide should point to the relevant offer, work proof, or project-start route.
