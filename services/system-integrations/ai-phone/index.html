System Integrations · AI Phone

Missed calls are not a personality problem. They are a system problem.

When the crew is on a job, in a service window, or mid-shift, the phone still rings. Voicemail is not a sales process. An AI phone layer can hold the line, collect the right details, and push the opportunity into the same path as a web form or chat lead.

Resonant builds these systems as part of system integrations — not as a disconnected product pitch. The website, forms, CRM, and phone should describe one customer journey.

What a serious AI phone setup can do

  • Greet callers with the business name and service area in plain language.
  • Answer FAQ-level questions: hours, service area, starting prices, prep, booking rules.
  • Qualify: job type, timing, address or city, budget range, urgency.
  • Route: hot leads to a human, routine requests to booking or CRM, after-hours to a clean summary queue.
  • Book when rules allow: calendar slots, buffer times, service windows.
  • Summarize: who called, what they need, what was promised, what to do next.

Why it belongs with the website

If the site says one thing and the phone says another, conversion drops. Field names, service names, cities, and next steps should match. Analytics should show call + form + chat as related demand, not three separate mysteries.
