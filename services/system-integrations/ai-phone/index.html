System Integrations · AI Phone

Missed calls are not a staffing problem. They are a money problem.

Every business that lives on the phone loses revenue when a real human cannot pick up. Voicemail is not a sales process. An AI phone layer answers, qualifies, and keeps the opportunity moving — continuous intake that passes through into booking, CRM, and the right person.

If you run a restaurant with OpenTable, online ordering, VIP requests, an executive chef’s line, and more than one location, one generic inbox is not enough. You need rules: who gets the reservation, who gets the vendor call, who gets the private-dining inquiry. The same is true for multi-location trades and service brands. The AI should never “miss” the call — it captures, routes, and logs so the business can act.

Resonant builds that as part of system integrations, including pass-through into ordering, numbering, CRM, and hospitality stacks when the operation needs it. Website, forms, chat, and phone describe one journey.

What a serious AI phone setup can do

  • Answer after hours and during peak service without killing the lead.
  • Qualify before a human steps in: need, timing, location, urgency.
  • Route correctly — host stand vs chef vs manager vs field tech vs VIP path.
  • Connect into booking, ordering, and CRM so the handoff is structured, not a voicemail dump.
  • Write notes and next steps so nothing valuable evaporates between shifts.

Why it belongs with the website and ops tools

If the site says one thing and the phone says another, conversion drops. Names, services, locations, and next steps should match. Analytics should treat call, form, and chat as related demand. For hospitality operators, that can include the same thinking we use around intelligent routing and floor systems — not a gadget bolted onto a broken process.
