Search Architecture

The site should explain itself clearly to both buyers and crawlers.

SEO is not just keywords. The foundation is whether the site has a clear topical map, useful pages, clean metadata, structured signals, fast-enough assets, and enough internal logic for search engines to understand what the business does.

That matters especially for service businesses. A homepage can introduce the company, but the deeper pages carry the practical search intent: the specific service, the area served, the proof, the questions, and the next action.

The baseline we build

  • Page map shaped around offers, industries, locations, and proof.
  • Titles and descriptions written for real intent, not generic stuffing.
  • Canonical URLs, Open Graph data, sitemap, robots file, and LLM-facing summary.
  • Organization, service, breadcrumb, and FAQ structured data where useful.
  • Analytics and launch checks so the site can move into a weekly SEO rhythm.

How this connects to growth

The launch foundation creates the base. After that, the site can move into an SEO cadence: new service pages, case studies, comparison content, local pages, FAQs, and proof updates based on what the business is actually trying to win.
