Hospitality Marketing · Ohio

A hospitality website has to sell the experience and support the operation.

Restaurants, bars, hotels, and venues do not need another pretty homepage that ignores service. Guests want menus, hours, reservations, rooms or events, private dining, catering, directions, and confidence. Operators need the site to capture demand, reduce friction, and report what is actually working.

Resonant builds those sites from an operator perspective. We also maintain free tools used on real floors — including Open Source Barware — and a longer hospitality systems direction at Intelligent Hospitality Systems. Those projects stay free of hard sell; they exist so operators can work. The marketing work on this site is separate: paid custom systems when a venue needs the web stack wired to how money moves.

What the build should include

  • Fast mobile pages for menus, reservations, events, rooms or packages, private dining, catering, and order flows.
  • Ohio-aware local SEO around cuisine, neighborhood, venue type, and high-intent services.
  • Google Business Profile support, review paths, analytics, and campaign tracking.
  • Booking, inquiry, and follow-up systems that route the right information to the right person.
  • AI phone, OpenTable or booking integrations, online ordering paths, and social support where they earn their keep.
  • Operational language that proves the venue is run by professionals, not just marketed by an agency.

Why floor experience changes the marketing

Most agencies can write restaurant copy. Fewer can speak clearly about inventory, vendor lock-in, beverage programs, shift realities, and why portal-juggling drains efficiency. That perspective changes the page map, CTAs, and proof — around how hospitality businesses actually make money in Ohio and beyond.
