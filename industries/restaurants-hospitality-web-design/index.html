Hospitality Marketing

A restaurant website has to sell the experience and support the operation.

Restaurants and bars do not need another pretty homepage that ignores the realities of service. Guests want menus, hours, reservations, events, private dining, catering, directions, and confidence. Operators need the site to capture demand, reduce friction, and report what is actually working.

That is why this page combines Resonant marketing with Open Source Barware and Intelligent Hospitality Systems. Open Source Barware is a free inventory program for bars: voice-walk counts, weekly process, variance tracking, optional invoice photo reading, no subscriptions, and no vendor lock-in. IHS extends that direction into full inventory management, vendor-agnostic hospitality operations, and unified supply-chain visibility.

What the restaurant build should include

  • Fast mobile pages for menus, reservations, events, private dining, catering, and gift-card or order flows.
  • Local SEO structure around cuisine, neighborhood, venue type, high-intent services, and search-friendly proof.
  • Google Business Profile support, review paths, analytics, and campaign tracking.
  • Booking, inquiry, and follow-up systems that route the right information to the right person.
  • Phone AI, OpenTable integrations, online ordering paths, and social media support.
  • Operational content that proves the venue is run by professionals, not just marketed by an agency.

Why the OSB and IHS connection matters

Most web agencies can write restaurant copy. Fewer can speak clearly about inventory, vendor lock-in, beverage programs, bar managers, shift realities, and why portal-juggling drains efficiency. That operating perspective changes the marketing: the page map, calls to action, service language, and proof are built around how hospitality businesses actually make money.
